ORGANIZATION

WORLD HEALTH ORGANIZATION

The World Health Organization (WHO) is a specialized agency of the United Nations responsible for international public health. Established on 7 April 1948, its primary role is to direct and coordinate global health efforts, set health standards, and provide technical assistance to countries. WHO's mission encompasses a wide range of health issues, including disease prevention, health promotion, and emergency response.

ZUELLIG FAMILY FOUNDATION

The Zuellig Family foundation is a specialized agency here in the Philipines. Accelerating access to improved public health services for all Filipinos. It aims to provide bridge from quality healthcare for the underprivileged. Alongisde Reforms tailored to the needs of the underprivileged. Working towards healthcare systems that bridge the gap between the privileged minority and the struggling majority in the Philippines.

INTERNATIONAL
UNICEF

UNIECF'S goal is to support nationwide vaccination campaigns (measles, polio, COVID-19). Provides therapeutic food to malnourished children. Improve maternal healthcare and nutrition for pregnant mothers And Ensures clean water and sanitation in schools and communities

Gavi, the Vaccine Alliance

GAVI's goal is to provide vaccines for measles, polio, HPV, and more. Help in developing countries to strengthen their immunization systems GAVI also played a major role in COVID-19 vaccine rollout. Protect millions of children from preventable diseases

The Global Fund to Fight AIDS, Tuberculosis and Malaria

Their mission is to fund life-saving treatments, create mosquito nets, testing kits, and vaccines Supports health systems nearly worldwide. One of the biggest contributors to lowering global HIV & TB deaths

WHO’s Global Polio Eradication
Initiative

WHO GPEI's goal is to provide oral polio vaccines to millions of children, tracks polio outbreaks using advanced surveillance, send rapid-response teams to prevent spread, and work with governments to strengthen routine immunization

LOCAL

Universal Health Care (UHC) Program by PhilHealth

UHC program by Philheath, enrolls all Filipinos in the national health insurance system. Also expands coverage for inpatient, outpatient, emergency, and preventive care. While strengthening the primary health care through local Health Care Provider Networks. And reducing out-of-pocket medical expenses

National Immunization Program (NIP) by DOH

The NIP by DOH aims and achieves to provide free vaccines for measles, polio, hepatitis B, tetanus, diphtheria, tuberculosis, rubella, and more. Conducts “catch-up immunization drives” nationwide Runs vaccination programs within schools(HPV, tetanus-diphtheria). And leads disease outbreak response and vaccination campaigns
